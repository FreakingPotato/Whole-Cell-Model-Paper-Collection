# Spatial organization of the flow of genetic information in bacteria

- Paper ID: `WCM-040`
- Year: 2010
- Journal: Nature
- DOI: 10.1038/nature09152
- Method class: Mechanistic models
- Organism: Escherichia coli
- PDF status: landing_page_only
- Modeled objects: Whole-cell integration, Gene-expression machinery, Spatial structure and dynamics, Modeling framework

## Overview
Shows that transcription and translation occupy organized spatial patterns in bacteria, challenging the assumption that the cytoplasm is well mixed.

## Abstract
Shows that transcription and translation occupy organized spatial patterns in bacteria, challenging the assumption that the cytoplasm is well mixed.

## Methods Summary
Spatial imaging and analysis of where RNA polymerase, transcripts, and ribosomes localize inside bacterial cells.

## Contribution
Provides empirical support for spatially resolved gene-expression modules in whole-cell models.

## Limitations
- Spatial patterns were measured in specific conditions and need generalization across growth states and species

## Future Work
- Use condition-specific localization data to drive dynamic spatial whole-cell simulations
