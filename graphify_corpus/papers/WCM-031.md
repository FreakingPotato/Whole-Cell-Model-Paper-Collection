- Paper ID: `WCM-031`

- Journal: Nature Reviews Microbiology
- DOI: 10.1038/nrmicro2391
- Method class: Mechanistic models
- Organism: General bacteria

## Overview
Argues that polymer entropy can itself help drive segregation of replicated bacterial chromosomes, offering a physical complement to active transport models.

## Abstract
Argues that polymer entropy can itself help drive segregation of replicated bacterial chromosomes, offering a physical complement to active transport models.

## Methods Summary
Conceptual and theoretical analysis of chromosome segregation using polymer-physics arguments.

## Contribution
Introduced an influential physical principle that later chromosome-organization models can encode or test.

## Limitations
- Entropy alone is unlikely to explain all organism-specific segregation behaviors without active protein factors
