# A Whole-Cell Computational Model Predicts Phenotype from Genotype

- Paper ID: `WCM-002`
- Year: 2012
- Journal: Cell
- DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2012.05.044
- Method class: Mechanistic models
- Organism: Mycoplasma genitalium
- PDF status: landing_page_only
- Modeled objects: Whole-cell integration, Minimal-cell platform, Modeling framework

## Overview
Introduces the first genome-scale whole-cell model of Mycoplasma genitalium by coupling many mechanistic submodels to predict phenotype from genotype.

## Abstract
Introduces the first genome-scale whole-cell model of Mycoplasma genitalium by coupling many mechanistic submodels to predict phenotype from genotype.

## Methods Summary
Integrated 28 cellular process modules with literature-derived parameters and simulation-based phenotype prediction.

## Contribution
Established the canonical modern whole-cell-modeling framework and made genotype-to-phenotype simulation a concrete systems-biology target.

## Limitations
- Built on sparse and heterogeneous data for one small bacterium, so many parameters and assumptions were only weakly constrained

## Future Work
