# Cross-evaluation of E. coli’s operon structures via a whole-cell model suggests alternative cellular benefits for low- versus high-expressing operons

- Paper ID: `WCM-008`
- Year: 2024
- Journal: Cell Systems
- DOI: 10.1016/j.cels.2024.02.002
- Method class: Hybrid architectures
- Organism: Escherichia coli
- PDF status: landing_page_only
- Modeled objects: Whole-cell integration, Modeling framework

## Overview
Uses an E. coli whole-cell model to test how different operon architectures alter cellular tradeoffs across low- and high-expression regimes.

## Abstract
Uses an E. coli whole-cell model to test how different operon architectures alter cellular tradeoffs across low- and high-expression regimes.

## Methods Summary
In silico operon rewiring experiments performed inside a mechanistic whole-cell simulation of E. coli.

## Contribution
Shows how whole-cell models can evaluate genome organization hypotheses that would be expensive to test exhaustively in vivo.

## Limitations
- Findings inherit the assumptions and calibration limits of the underlying E. coli whole-cell model

## Future Work
- Pair simulation-guided operon redesign with experimental genome engineering to validate predicted benefits
